    Kiki's Delivery Service

    Stats

    Released: July 29, 1989
    Preceded by: My Neighbor Totoro
    Directed by: Hayao Miyazaki
    Followed by: Princess Mononoke
    Produced by: Yasuyoshi Tokuma
    Written by: Hayao Miyazaki
    Runtime: 102 minutes

    Kiki's Delivery Service (魔女の宅急便, Majo no Takkyūbin, literally "Witch's Delivery Service"), is a 1989 animated fantasy film written, directed and produced by Hayao Miyazaki. It was animated by Studio Ghibli for publisher Tokuma Shoten, Yamato Transport Co. and the Nippon Television Network and distributed by the Toei Company.

    The film is based on the children's novel of the same name by Eiko Kadono. However, the film deviated from the original novel's story and themes, which upset Kadono during its production. She has since reconciled with Miyazaki.[1] A more faithful live action film adaptation was released on March 1, 2014, featuring Kadono as the narrator.

    The film began production on April 1, 1988, and was released in Japan on July 29, 1989, and won the Animage Anime Grand Prix prize. It was the first film released under a 15-year distribution partnership between The Walt Disney Company and Studio Ghibli; Buena Vista Home Video recorded an English dub in 1997, which premiered in United States theaters at the Seattle International Film Festival on May 23, 1998. The film was released on home video in the U.S. and Canada on September 15, 1998.

    Plot

    One blustery day, thirteen-year-old witch-in-training Kiki lies idle on a field listening to her dad's radio when she hears an announcement that the evening would be clear with a beautiful full moon. She rushes home and informs her mother, Kokiri, that she plans to leave home that very evening instead of the following month as she had originally planned. As Kiki rushes to her room to pack, Kokiri informs her client of the age-old tradition of 13-year-old witches leaving home for a year on the night of a full moon to pursue their skill through training. As Kiki packs her belongings upstairs, her companion, a talking black cat named Jiji, asks why they can't wait till next month as they planned. Kiki tells him that if she meets a boy or something else occurs, she might not leave at all. Her father, Okino, arrives home and learns of Kiki's plans to depart that night, which cancels their camping trip that weekend. He begins calling relatives to inform them Kiki is leaving and soon, Kokiri fits Kiki with her purple witch's uniform.

    That evening, her family and friends gather to send Kiki off. Kokiri gives Kiki her broom, though at first Kiki is reluctant to take it. Her mother assures her this broom guided her to this town and it will guide Kiki. She leaves home with Jiji as her parents and family wishes her well. Along the way, she encounters a fortune-telling witch who tells her of the challenges she may face when settling in a new town. They part ways as she has completed her one year of training and is returning to her home town. Eventually it starts to rain, and soon Kiki and Jiji land in a train's box car where they spend the night.

    The next morning, the rain clears up, so Kiki flies on her broomstick to the port city of Koriko. The citizens, however, seem unfazed by her arrival. A police officer questions her reckless flying, but he gets distracted, giving Kiki a chance to leave. While trying to find somewhere to live, Kiki is pursued by Tombo Kopoli, a geeky boy obsessed with aviation who admires her flying ability. Kiki refuses to talk to a stranger, although she thanks Tombo for getting her out of trouble. After a day of searching for lodging, Kiki soon finds herself at a bakery owned by Osono, the kindly and heavily pregnant owner, who seems completely overwhelmed by the number of customers. After helping Osono return a pacifier to a baby, the two have a talk and Osono learns that Kiki currently had nowhere to stay. She offers Kiki a small apartment over their storage area to stay in and Kiki gratefully accepts.

    In exchange for accommodation, Kiki helps Osono mind the store. She confesses that her only skill is flying, so they decide to open a "Witch Delivery Business", delivering goods by broomstick. Kiki works hard at the store and soon they get the place all fixed up. One of Osono's regular customers named Maki notices Kiki and asks her to deliver a birthday gift for her nephew, Ket. Kiki gladly accepts and before leaving, she is spotted ascending into the air by Tombo, who wishes he could fly like her.

    Unfortunately, her first delivery goes badly as she is caught by a strong gust of wind and loses the black cat toy she is supposed to deliver. Since Jiji looks so similar to the toy cat, Kiki has him pretend to be the toy until she can find the real one. While at the young boy's house, Jiji is under the watchful eye of the family dog, Jeff.

    Meanwhile, Kiki finds the toy cat in the cabin of a young painter, Ursula. Noticing that the toy is ripped, Ursula offers to mend it in exchange for Kiki cleaning her floor. Once it is fixed, Ursula returns the doll and Kiki hurries off to save Jiji. They are able to make the switch easily with the help of Jeff and return home.

    As customers for her delivery service slows to a trickle Kiki falls into a slump. She reluctantly accepts a party invitation from Tombo and his friends from the Aviation Club. She arrives at the home of her next delivery appointment, an elderly woman who had been trying to make a herring and pumpkin potpie for her granddaughter's birthday. When the electric oven doesn't work, Kiki offers the woman help with her wood-burning oven in order to make the pie. The woman remembers that her clock runs a few minutes slow which, and Kiki realizes she will be late for Tombo's party. The Potpie is ready so Kiki rushes off to deliver it. On route, it begins to rain heavily as she delivers the potpie for the woman's granddaughter. When she arrives to the young girl's house, Kiki is stunned when she expresses her loathing of the potpie but accepts the gift reluctantly. Kiki makes her way home in silence through the rain. Exhausted from the journey, soaking wet from the rain, and having missed the party, Kiki soon falls ill with a fever.

    When she recovers, Osono clandestinely arranges for Kiki to see Tombo again by assigning her a delivery addressed to him. After Kiki apologizes for missing the party, Tombo shows off the new dirigible that arrived in town for repairs. He takes her for a test ride on the flying machine he built from a bicycle. Kiki warms up to Tombo but is intimidated by his petty friends, who seem to look down at her. Upset, she walks home in defeat, despite Tombo trying to be kind.

    Kiki's slump is worsened when discovers she can no longer understand Jiji, who has befriended a pretty white cat living next door. Kiki realizes that she is also losing her flying ability and witch powers, and she is forced to suspend her delivery business until she gets back her flying powers. She also tells Tombo over the phone she won't see him anymore out of sadness. Osono becomes worried about her. Kiki has a surprise visit from Ursula at the bakery, who after hearing what happened determines that Kiki's crisis is a form of artist's block and offers her time away at her cabin in order to relax. There, Kiki sees that Ursula was working on her latest painting, inspired by her encounter with Kiki. Later that night, Ursula talks about how she had gone through a similar situation, but with her art rather than powers. Ursula solved her problem by realizing that she had to find her own art style, rather than copying others. She suggests that if Kiki can find a new purpose in life, she will regain her powers.

    The following morning, Kiki visits the old woman. She personally wanted to thank Kiki for her hard work when she was last at the house and presents a cake she had in gratitude. At the same time, the television reports that the dirigible "The Spirit of Freedom" is set to depart. As the "Seahorse Band" and crowd bid the airship farewell, a sudden gale dislodges the airship's moors and it is blown off course, with Tombo hanging from the rope under the airship. The airship manages to crash into the clock tower and is suspended above the town plaza. Kiki then witnesses the Tombo hanging from one of the drifting vessel's mooring lines.

    Kiki takes a street sweeper's push-broom and, miraculously regaining her flying power, flies towards Tombo, who remains in danger even as the airship settles between two buildings. Kiki flies as fast as she can and, after some initial struggling with keeping the broom in place, manages to rescue Tombo as he slips and falls. During the celebration, Kiki rejoins with Jiji who hops on her shoulder and is once again speaking. She regains her confidence and resumes her delivery service. She also joins the aviation club. Osono gives birth to a healthy baby and Jiji becomes the father of four kittens. The story ends when Kiki writes a letter home saying that she and Jiji are happy and doing well with her witch skills.
